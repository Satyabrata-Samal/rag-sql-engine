# Metrics Definitions for Chinook Music Store

## Revenue Metrics

### Total Revenue
**Definition:** Sum of all invoice totals across all customers.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT SUM(total) as total_revenue
FROM invoices;
```

**Business Use:** Overall company revenue. Used in financial reports and forecasting.

**Example:** If you have invoices for $100, $150, $200, total revenue = $450

---

### Revenue per Customer
**Definition:** Total revenue generated from a customer divided by the number of unique customers.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT 
    SUM(i.total) / COUNT(DISTINCT i.customer_id) as revenue_per_customer
FROM invoices i;
```

**Business Use:** Average spending per customer. Shows customer value.

**Alternative (per specific customer):**
```sql
SELECT 
    customer_id,
    SUM(total) as customer_lifetime_value
FROM invoices
GROUP BY customer_id
ORDER BY customer_lifetime_value DESC;
```

---

### Monthly Revenue
**Definition:** Total revenue generated in a specific calendar month.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT 
    DATE_TRUNC('month', invoice_date) as month,
    SUM(total) as monthly_revenue
FROM invoices
GROUP BY DATE_TRUNC('month', invoice_date)
ORDER BY month DESC;
```

**Business Use:** Track revenue trends month-over-month. Identify seasonal patterns.

---

### Average Order Value (AOV)
**Definition:** Total revenue divided by total number of invoices.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT 
    SUM(total) / COUNT(*) as average_order_value
FROM invoices;
```

**Business Use:** Understand typical transaction size. Used in marketing ROI calculations.

---

## Customer Metrics

### Total Customers
**Definition:** Count of unique customers in the database.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT COUNT(*) as total_customers
FROM customers;
```

**Business Use:** Company size metrics. Baseline for customer acquisition.

---

### Active Customers
**Definition:** Customers with at least one purchase in the last 90 days.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT customer_id) as active_customers
FROM invoices
WHERE invoice_date >= CURRENT_DATE - INTERVAL '90 days';
```

**Business Use:** Understand current customer engagement. Track growth/decline.

---

### Churn (Inactive Customers)
**Definition:** Customers with NO invoices in the last 90 days.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT COUNT(*) as churned_customers
FROM customers
WHERE customer_id NOT IN (
    SELECT DISTINCT customer_id
    FROM invoices
    WHERE invoice_date >= CURRENT_DATE - INTERVAL '90 days'
);
```

**Business Use:** Identify at-risk customers. Track retention. Plan win-back campaigns.

**Note:** This is a simplified definition. In reality, you'd also exclude:
- Customers acquired < 90 days ago
- Trial customers
- Free-tier customers

---

### Churn Rate
**Definition:** Number of churned customers divided by total customers.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT 
    COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN customer_id NOT IN (
        SELECT DISTINCT customer_id
        FROM invoices
        WHERE invoice_date >= CURRENT_DATE - INTERVAL '90 days'
    ) THEN customer_id END) * 100.0 / COUNT(*) as churn_rate_percent
FROM customers;
```

**Business Use:** Key retention metric. Benchmark against industry. Track quarter-over-quarter.

---

### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
**Definition:** Total revenue generated by a customer over their entire relationship with the company.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT 
    c.customer_id,
    c.first_name,
    c.last_name,
    SUM(i.total) as customer_lifetime_value
FROM customers c
LEFT JOIN invoices i ON c.customer_id = i.customer_id
GROUP BY c.customer_id, c.first_name, c.last_name
ORDER BY customer_lifetime_value DESC;
```

**Business Use:** Identify most valuable customers. Prioritize retention efforts. Segment by value.

---

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
**Definition:** Total marketing spend divided by number of new customers acquired.

**How to Calculate:**

**Note:** Chinook database doesn't have marketing spend data, so we use:

**Simplified (using average spend per customer):**
```sql
SELECT 
    AVG(total) as estimated_cac_per_customer
FROM invoices;
```

**Business Use:** Evaluate marketing efficiency. Determine if customer is profitable.

---

### CAC Payback Period
**Definition:** Number of months until revenue from a customer exceeds their acquisition cost.

**How to Calculate:**

**Simplified Example (for Chinook):**
```sql
WITH customer_monthly_revenue AS (
    SELECT 
        customer_id,
        EXTRACT(YEAR_MONTH FROM invoice_date) as month,
        SUM(total) as monthly_total
    FROM invoices
    GROUP BY customer_id, EXTRACT(YEAR_MONTH FROM invoice_date)
)
SELECT 
    AVG(monthly_total) as avg_monthly_revenue
FROM customer_monthly_revenue;
```

**Business Use:** Determine if a customer is worthwhile to acquire. Plan sales budgets.

---

## Product Metrics

### Total Products
**Definition:** Count of unique products/tracks in the catalog.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT COUNT(*) as total_products
FROM tracks;
```

**Business Use:** Catalog size. Understanding product breadth.

---

### Product Adoption Rate
**Definition:** Percentage of customers who have purchased a specific product.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT 
    t.track_id,
    t.name as track_name,
    COUNT(DISTINCT il.customer_id) as customers_purchased,
    ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(DISTINCT il.customer_id) / 
        (SELECT COUNT(*) FROM customers), 2) as adoption_rate_percent
FROM tracks t
LEFT JOIN invoice_items il ON t.track_id = il.track_id
GROUP BY t.track_id, t.name
ORDER BY adoption_rate_percent DESC;
```

**Business Use:** Identify popular products. Find niche products. Guide marketing efforts.

---

### Best Selling Products
**Definition:** Products with highest total revenue.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT 
    t.name as product_name,
    COUNT(*) as times_purchased,
    SUM(il.unit_price * il.quantity) as total_revenue
FROM tracks t
JOIN invoice_items il ON t.track_id = il.track_id
GROUP BY t.name
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC
LIMIT 10;
```

**Business Use:** Identify revenue drivers. Plan inventory/production. Pricing decisions.

---

## Geographical Metrics

### Revenue by Country
**Definition:** Total revenue grouped by customer country.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT 
    c.country,
    COUNT(DISTINCT c.customer_id) as num_customers,
    SUM(i.total) as total_revenue,
    AVG(i.total) as avg_order_value
FROM customers c
LEFT JOIN invoices i ON c.customer_id = i.customer_id
GROUP BY c.country
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC;
```

**Business Use:** Identify top markets. Plan regional expansion. Localization decisions.

---

### Customer Distribution by Country
**Definition:** Number of customers per country.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT 
    country,
    COUNT(*) as num_customers,
    ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(*) / (SELECT COUNT(*) FROM customers), 2) as percent_of_total
FROM customers
GROUP BY country
ORDER BY num_customers DESC;
```

**Business Use:** Market penetration. Regional growth opportunities.

---

## Trend Metrics

### Month-over-Month Growth
**Definition:** Percentage change in a metric from one month to the next.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
WITH monthly_revenue AS (
    SELECT 
        DATE_TRUNC('month', invoice_date) as month,
        SUM(total) as revenue
    FROM invoices
    GROUP BY DATE_TRUNC('month', invoice_date)
)
SELECT 
    month,
    revenue,
    LAG(revenue) OVER (ORDER BY month) as previous_month_revenue,
    ROUND(100.0 * (revenue - LAG(revenue) OVER (ORDER BY month)) / 
        LAG(revenue) OVER (ORDER BY month), 2) as mom_growth_percent
FROM monthly_revenue
ORDER BY month DESC;
```

**Business Use:** Spot trends. Identify growth/decline periods. Forecast.

---

### Customer Growth
**Definition:** New customers added per month.

**How to Calculate:**
```sql
SELECT 
    DATE_TRUNC('month', created_at) as signup_month,
    COUNT(*) as new_customers
FROM customers
GROUP BY DATE_TRUNC('month', created_at)
ORDER BY signup_month DESC;
```

**Note:** If `created_at` doesn't exist in Chinook, use `first_invoice_date` instead.

**Business Use:** Track acquisition. Plan sales team capacity. Forecast growth.
