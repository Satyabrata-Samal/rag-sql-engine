## Business Model

### What is Chinook?

Chinook is a digital media store database. The Chinook data model represents a digital media store, including tables for artists, albums, media tracks, invoices, and customers. Media-related data was created using real data from an iTunes library.

### Key Characteristics

- **Business Type:** Digital music store
- **Products:** Music tracks (songs) sold individually or in albums
- **Sales Model:** One-time digital purchases (not subscription)
- **Geography:** Multi-national with customers from multiple countries
- **Employees:** Sales support representatives managing customer relationships
- **Organization:** Hierarchical reporting structure (managers oversee employees)

### Revenue Streams

The company generates revenue from individual track and album purchases through invoices.

## Core Definitions

### Revenue Metrics

#### Total Revenue
- **Definition:** Sum of all `invoice.total` amounts
- **Calculation:** `SUM(invoice.total)`
- **Time Period:** All invoices or specific date range
- **Exclusions:** None documented (all invoices are net revenue)
- **What It Tells You:** Total money the company earned from sales

#### Revenue by Country
- **Definition:** Total revenue from customers in each country
- **Key Column:** `customer.country` (customer location, not billing country)
- **Use Case:** Geographic market analysis, regional performance
- **SQL Pattern:**
```sql
SELECT c.country, SUM(i.total) as total_revenue
FROM customer c
JOIN invoice i ON c.customer_id = i.customer_id
GROUP BY c.country
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC;
```

#### Revenue by Genre
- **Definition:** Total revenue from each music genre
- **Requires:** Joining through: customer → invoice → invoice_line → track → genre
- **Use Case:** Identify popular music genres, inventory decisions
- **Important:** Monitoring genre trends can help improving inventory management decisions

#### Sales Per Unit Per Genre
- **Definition:** Average sale price or unit count per genre
- **Use Case:** Pricing strategy, inventory allocation

#### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
- **Definition:** Total revenue generated by a customer over their entire history
- **Calculation:** `SUM(invoice.total)` per customer
- **Use Case:** Incentives should be given to top customers to motivate and encourage them to keep patronizing the company

### Customer Metrics

#### Best Customer
- **Definition:** The customer who has spent the most money
- **Metric:** Highest total spend across all invoices
- **Use Case:** VIP targeting, loyalty programs

#### Customer Segmentation
- **By Geography:** Countries with most invoices
- **By Value:** High-value vs low-value customers
- **By Preference:** By segmenting customers based on their music preferences, campaigns can be tailored for improved effectiveness

#### Genre Preference Analysis
- **Definition:** Identify which genres each customer listens to
- **Finding:** All 59 customers in the database have listened to Rock Music
- **Insight:** Rock is the most popular genre across all customers

## Data Quality & Limitations

### Known Limitations

#### 1. Historical Data Only
- **Issue:** Data spans 2009-2013 only (5 years)
- **Implication:** Cannot use for current business decisions without understanding historical context
- **Use:** Training database, not production data

#### 2. No Active Churn Tracking
- **Issue:** No explicit churn reason or cancellation field
- **Workaround:** Infer churn from lack of recent purchases
- **Assumption:** No purchase in defined period = customer lost

#### 3. No Refund Tracking
- **Issue:** Database only contains successful sales, no refunds/returns
- **Implication:** All `invoice.total` values are positive (net revenue)
- **Check:** No negative values should exist in `invoice.total`

#### 4. No Subscription Data
- **Issue:** All sales are one-time purchases (no recurring revenue)
- **Implication:** Cannot calculate lifetime value for subscriptions
- **Use:** Best for analyzing product purchase behavior

#### 5. Limited Customer Context
- **Issue:** No signup/acquisition date field
- **Workaround:** Use `MIN(invoice_date)` per customer as approximate signup date
- **Limitation:** Doesn't account for customers who never purchased

#### 6. No Marketing Data
- **Issue:** No source of acquisition, marketing spend, or campaign information
- **Impact:** Cannot calculate CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- **Impact:** Cannot perform attribution analysis

#### 7. Fictional Customer Data
- **Issue:** Customer names and addresses are fictitious (good for training)
- **Implication:** Cannot use for production decisions
- **Note:** Generated using Google Maps addresses for realism

## Important Assumptions

### General Assumptions

1. **All invoices are valid sales** - no deductions or cancellations tracked
2. **One invoice = one transaction** (may contain multiple line items)
3. **Prices are in USD** - no currency conversions needed
4. **No inventory management** - assume all products are available
5. **Geographic accuracy:** Use `customer.country` not `billing_country`

### For Analysis

1. **Customer identity:** Use `customer_id` as primary identifier
2. **Time reference:** Use `invoice_date` for all temporal analysis
3. **Revenue accuracy:** Trust `invoice.total` as source of truth
4. **Product pricing:** Use `invoice_line.unit_price` (price at time of sale), not `track.unit_price` (current price)

### For Business Insights

1. Feedback should be demanded from the customers as to why they prefer listening to Rock Music to other types of music
2. More publicity and promotions should be made for other genres of music to attract more customers thereby increasing the company's revenue
3. All artists who have written rock music should be celebrated as they contribute a large percentage to the company's revenue

## Sample Queries

### Query 1: Revenue by Country
```sql
SELECT 
    c.country,
    COUNT(i.invoice_id) as num_invoices,
    SUM(i.total) as total_revenue,
    AVG(i.total) as avg_invoice_value
FROM customer c
JOIN invoice i ON c.customer_id = i.customer_id
GROUP BY c.country
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC;
```

### Query 2: Top Customers by Spending
```sql
SELECT 
    c.customer_id,
    c.first_name || ' ' || c.last_name as customer_name,
    c.country,
    SUM(i.total) as lifetime_value,
    COUNT(i.invoice_id) as num_purchases
FROM customer c
JOIN invoice i ON c.customer_id = i.customer_id
GROUP BY c.customer_id, c.first_name, c.last_name, c.country
ORDER BY lifetime_value DESC
LIMIT 10;
```

### Query 3: Genre Popularity
```sql
SELECT 
    g.name as genre,
    COUNT(DISTINCT i.invoice_id) as num_purchases,
    SUM(il.unit_price * il.quantity) as total_revenue
FROM genre g
JOIN track t ON g.genre_id = t.genre_id
JOIN invoice_line il ON t.track_id = il.track_id
JOIN invoice i ON il.invoice_id = i.invoice_id
GROUP BY g.name
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC;
```

### Query 4: Rock Music Customers
```sql
SELECT DISTINCT 
    c.email,
    c.first_name,
    c.last_name,
    g.name as genre
FROM customer c
JOIN invoice i ON c.customer_id = i.customer_id
JOIN invoice_line il ON i.invoice_id = il.invoice_id
JOIN track t ON il.track_id = t.track_id
JOIN genre g ON t.genre_id = g.genre_id
WHERE g.name = 'Rock'
ORDER BY c.email;
```

### Query 5: Most Prolific Rock Artists
```sql
SELECT 
    a.name as artist,
    COUNT(t.track_id) as rock_tracks
FROM artist a
JOIN album al ON a.artist_id = al.artist_id
JOIN track t ON al.album_id = t.album_id
JOIN genre g ON t.genre_id = g.genre_id
WHERE g.name = 'Rock'
GROUP BY a.name
ORDER BY rock_tracks DESC
LIMIT 10;
```

### Query 6: Top Artist by Revenue
```sql
SELECT 
    a.name as artist,
    SUM(il.unit_price * il.quantity) as total_revenue,
    COUNT(DISTINCT i.customer_id) as num_customers
FROM artist a
JOIN album al ON a.artist_id = al.artist_id
JOIN track t ON al.album_id = t.album_id
JOIN invoice_line il ON t.track_id = il.track_id
JOIN invoice i ON il.invoice_id = i.invoice_id
GROUP BY a.name
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC;
```

### Query 7: Invoices by Year
```sql
SELECT 
    YEAR(invoice_date) as year,
    COUNT(*) as num_invoices,
    SUM(total) as total_sales,
    AVG(total) as avg_sale_amount
FROM invoice
GROUP BY YEAR(invoice_date)
ORDER BY year;
```

### Query 8: Sales Support Rep Performance
```sql
SELECT 
    e.employee_id,
    e.first_name || ' ' || e.last_name as employee_name,
    COUNT(DISTINCT c.customer_id) as num_customers,
    SUM(i.total) as total_customer_revenue
FROM employee e
LEFT JOIN customer c ON e.employee_id = c.support_rep_id
LEFT JOIN invoice i ON c.customer_id = i.customer_id
GROUP BY e.employee_id, e.first_name, e.last_name
ORDER BY num_customers DESC;
```
